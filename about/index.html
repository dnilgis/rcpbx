About rcpbx

rcpbx (recipe box) is a personal collection of 153 tested recipes. No life stories. No ads. No popups. No newsletter signup modals. Just the recipe.

Every recipe here has been cooked, adjusted, and documented. Sources are credited where applicable—this is a curated collection, not original creations.

Why this exists: Recipe websites have become unbearable. You want to know how to make mashed potatoes, but first you have to scroll past 2,000 words about someone's grandmother's kitchen and dodge three popup ads. This is the antidote.

Features:

  • Clickable timers in recipe steps
  • Tap ingredients to toggle metric/imperial
  • "Keep screen on" for cooking mode
  • Print-friendly recipe cards with QR codes
  • Dark mode support
  • No tracking beyond basic analytics

Built with: Plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No frameworks, no build process, no dependencies. Hosted on GitHub Pages.

Philosophy: Every element serves someone actively cooking. No theoretical content. No wasted space. Just practical utility.

Questions? Find a bug? Open an issue on GitHub.
