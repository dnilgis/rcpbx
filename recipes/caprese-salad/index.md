# Caprese Salad

Tomato, mozzarella, basil. The holy trinity.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 0 min · Serves: 4

_Tested 2026-07. Verified as written. The only failure mode is bad tomatoes._

## Ingredients

- 3 large ripe tomatoes
- 1 lb fresh mozzarella (not the shredded kind)
- 1 bunch fresh basil
- 3 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- Flaky sea salt
- Fresh cracked black pepper
- Balsamic glaze (optional)

## Steps

1. Slice tomatoes and mozzarella into 1/4-inch thick rounds.
2. Arrange alternating slices of tomato and mozzarella on a platter, slightly overlapping.
3. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the slices.
4. Drizzle generously with olive oil.
5. Season with flaky salt and cracked pepper.
6. If using, drizzle balsamic glaze in a zigzag pattern.
7. Serve immediately at room temperature.

## Notes

- Use room temperature tomatoes - cold kills the flavor.
- Fresh mozzarella (the soft kind in water) is non-negotiable.
- This is a summer dish. Don't bother with winter tomatoes.

## When it goes wrong

- Tastes like nothing → cold or out-of-season tomatoes; room temperature, peak summer, or make something else.
- Watery platter → mozzarella not drained; let slices sit on paper towels 10 minutes.
- Balsamic drowned it → glaze is optional for a reason; a zigzag, not a pour.
