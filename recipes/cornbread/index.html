Southern Cornbread

Cast iron, crispy edges, no sugar. The way it should be.

Prep 10 min
Cook 25 min
Serves 8

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups cornmeal (stone-ground if you can find it)
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1½ cups buttermilk
  • 1 egg
  • 10 tbsp butter, divided

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Place 10-inch cast iron skillet in oven while it heats.
  2. Whisk cornmeal, flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in large bowl.
  3. Melt 6 tablespoons butter. Whisk with buttermilk and egg in another bowl.
  4. Pour wet ingredients into dry. Stir until just combined. Don't overmix.
  5. Carefully remove hot skillet from oven. Add remaining 4 tablespoons butter, swirl to coat.
  6. Immediately pour batter into sizzling butter. It should sizzle loudly.
  7. Bake 20-25 minutes until golden brown and toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Brush top with more butter if desired. Let cool 5 minutes before slicing.

Notes

HOT skillet + butter = crispy crust: This is non-negotiable. The sizzle when batter hits the pan creates the crust.

No sugar: Southern cornbread is savory. If you want sweet, that's Northern-style (and that's fine, just different).

Cast iron matters: Retains heat, creates crispy edges. A regular pan won't give the same results.

Bacon grease: For extra flavor, replace some butter with bacon drippings.

Serve with: Chili, beans, collard greens, or crumbled into buttermilk (yes, really).
