Smash Burgers

Crispy lacy edges, diner-style perfection. Fast and simple.

Prep 5 min
Cook 10 min
Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef chuck (80/20)
  • 8 slices American cheese
  • 4 burger buns (potato buns work great)
  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper
  • 2 tbsp butter for buns

Suggested Toppings

  • Pickle slices
  • Thinly sliced onion
  • Ketchup, mustard, mayo
  • Shredded lettuce

Steps

  1. Divide beef into 8 equal balls (2 oz each). Don't pack them tight—keep them loose.
  2. Heat cast iron skillet or griddle over HIGH heat until smoking hot. This is crucial.
  3. Place 2-4 balls on the hot surface (don't crowd). Immediately smash flat with a sturdy spatula. Press HARD for 10 seconds. Should be very thin.
  4. Season the top with salt and pepper AFTER smashing (not before).
  5. Cook 2 minutes until edges are brown and crispy. Flip.
  6. Immediately add cheese slice. Cook 1 more minute.
  7. Stack 2 patties per burger.
  8. Toast buns in the beef fat on the same pan. Assemble and serve immediately.

Notes

Why season AFTER smashing: Salt draws moisture from raw meat. Salting after = better crust.

American cheese: Melts better than anything else. This is not the time for fancy cheese.

The smash: Press as thin as possible. The goal is maximum surface contact = maximum crust.

Why 2 thin patties: More crust per burger. That crust is the whole point.

Ventilation: This creates smoke. Open windows or turn on the fan.
