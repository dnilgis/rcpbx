# Buldak Carbonara

Korean fire noodles, tamed with yolk and parmesan.

Prep: 5 min · Cook: 10 min · Serves: 2

**Verdict: WORTH IT** — The rare stunt-food recipe that's actually a good bowl of noodles.

_Tested 2026-07. One sauce packet plus the dairy is the correct default; the full two-packet version is a challenge video, not dinner._

## Ingredients

- 2 packs Samyang buldak ramen (original or carbonara flavor)
- 2 large egg yolks
- 40g (1/2 cup) finely grated parmesan
- 1/4 cup (60ml) heavy cream
- 1 Tbsp butter
- 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 green onions, sliced
- Toasted sesame seeds

## Steps

1. Boil the noodles 4 minutes (discard nothing yet - you need the water). Reserve 1/2 cup noodle water, then drain.
2. Whisk egg yolks, parmesan, and cream in a bowl. This is your rescue squad.
3. Melt butter in the empty pot over medium-low, cook garlic 30 seconds until fragrant, not brown.
4. Add drained noodles, ONE sauce packet (start there - two is a commitment), and a splash of noodle water. Toss to coat.
5. Kill the heat. Wait 30 seconds - a raging-hot pot scrambles the yolks.
6. Pour in the yolk mixture, tossing constantly, adding noodle water a splash at a time until the sauce turns glossy and clings.
7. Top with green onions, sesame seeds, and the flavor-flake packet if your pack has one.

## Notes

- The heat math: one packet with the dairy lands around 'very spicy but conversational.' Two packets is the full 2x challenge with a cream buffer.
- Off the heat is the whole technique - carbonara logic applies whether it's guanciale or fire sauce.
- The buldak 'carbonara' flavor pack is fine, but building it from original + real yolk and parmesan tastes dramatically better.

## When it goes wrong

- Scrambled, grainy sauce → pot was still screaming when the yolks went in; off heat, 30 seconds, keep tossing.
- Sauce too thick and gluey → needs noodle water; loosen a splash at a time.
- Inedibly hot → you used both packets; next time start with one and add.
- Sauce won't cling → pre-grated shelf parmesan doesn't melt; grate a wedge.
