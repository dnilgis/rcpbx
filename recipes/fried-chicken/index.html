Southern Fried Chicken

Buttermilk-brined, shatteringly crispy. Worth every step.

Prep 30 min
Brine 6+ hrs
Serves 4

Ingredients

Buttermilk Brine

  • 3-4 lbs chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, breasts)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne

Dredge

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper

For Frying

  • Vegetable oil or peanut oil (enough for 1" depth in pan)

Steps

  1. Brine: Whisk buttermilk, salt, paprika, garlic powder, and cayenne. Submerge chicken pieces. Refrigerate at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.
  2. Mix dredge: Combine flour, cornstarch, paprika, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and pepper in a wide bowl.
  3. Double dredge: Remove chicken from buttermilk (don't shake off excess). Coat in flour mixture. Dip back in buttermilk. Coat in flour again. Let sit on rack 10 minutes.
  4. Heat oil to 350°F in Dutch oven or deep skillet. Use a thermometer.
  5. Fry in batches (don't crowd): Dark meat 13-15 minutes, breasts 15-18 minutes. Flip halfway. Internal temp should reach 165°F.
  6. Drain on wire rack (not paper towels—they make it soggy). Season with pinch of salt immediately.

Notes

Cornstarch = extra crispy: It creates a shatteringly crunchy coating. Don't skip it.

Double dredge: The second coating is what gives you that craggy, crispy exterior.

Don't crowd: Too much chicken drops oil temp = greasy, soggy coating.

Wire rack: Draining on paper towels traps steam and makes bottom soggy.

Storage: Best fresh. Reheat in 400°F oven on wire rack to re-crisp.
