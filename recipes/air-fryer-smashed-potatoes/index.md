# Air Fryer Smashed Potatoes

Boiled, smashed, blasted. Maximum crust per potato.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 35 min · Serves: 4

**Verdict: WORTH IT** — The correct answer to 'what do I make with these baby potatoes' from now on.

_Tested 2026-07. Fork-tender before smashing and a real steam-dry are the two non-negotiables._

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 lb (680g) baby Yukon Gold potatoes
- 2 Tbsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt (for the boiling water)
- 3 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Flaky salt and chopped parsley or chives, to finish

## Steps

1. Cover potatoes with cold water by an inch, add the 2 Tbsp salt, and boil until a fork slides in with zero resistance, 15-18 minutes. Undercooked potatoes don't smash, they break.
2. Drain and let them steam-dry in the colander 5 minutes - surface moisture is the enemy of crust.
3. On a cutting board, press each potato to about 1/2 inch thick with the bottom of a glass or measuring cup. Ragged edges are the goal.
4. Mix olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Brush or spoon over both sides of each smashed potato - get into the crags.
5. Air fry at 400°F (200°C) in a single layer (batches if needed), 15-18 minutes, flipping once halfway, until deep golden and crackling at the edges.
6. Finish with flaky salt and herbs. Serve hot - the crunch has a half-life of about 15 minutes.

## Notes

- Yukon Golds are creamy inside; red potatoes work, russets fall apart.
- Salting the boil like pasta water seasons the potato all the way through - the only chance you get.
- Oven version: 450°F on a preheated sheet pan, 25-30 minutes, same flip.

## When it goes wrong

- Potatoes fell apart when smashed → overboiled or pressed too thin; fork-tender, half an inch.
- No crunch → crowded basket or wet potatoes; single layer, steam-dry first.
- Stuck to the basket → dry basket; the oil goes on the potatoes AND a light mist on the basket.
- Bland → seasoned only at the end; the salt in the boiling water is doing the real work.
