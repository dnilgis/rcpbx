# Overnight Oats

Prep tonight, grab and go tomorrow. Breakfast that makes itself.

Prep: 5 min · Cook: 0 min (8 hours soak) · Serves: 1

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
- 1/2 cup milk (any kind)
- 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
- 1 Tbsp chia seeds (optional)
- 1 Tbsp maple syrup or honey
- 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt
- Toppings: fresh fruit, nuts, nut butter, granola

## Steps

1. Combine oats, milk, yogurt, chia seeds, maple syrup, vanilla, and salt in a jar or container.
2. Stir well to combine. Make sure all oats are submerged.
3. Cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours or overnight.
4. In the morning, stir. Add more milk if too thick.
5. Top with fresh fruit, nuts, or other toppings and eat cold, or microwave 1-2 minutes if you prefer it warm.

## Notes

- Oat type: Use old-fashioned rolled oats. Quick oats get too mushy. Steel cut won't soften enough.
- Ratio: The 1:1 ratio of oats to liquid is key. Adjust to your preferred thickness.
- Chia seeds: Not essential but add protein and help thicken. Flax works too.
- Meal prep: Make 5 jars on Sunday. They last up to 5 days in the fridge.
- Flavor combos: PB&J (peanut butter + jam), Banana Nut (banana + walnuts + cinnamon), Berry Blast (mixed berries + honey), Apple Pie (diced apple + cinnamon + pecans).
