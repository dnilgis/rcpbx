# Shakshuka

Eggs poached in spiced tomato sauce. One skillet, all the flavor.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 25 min · Serves: 4

## Ingredients

- 2 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (optional)
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 6 large eggs
- 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
- Fresh cilantro or parsley for serving
- Crusty bread for serving

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and bell pepper. Sauté 5-7 minutes until softened.
2. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in crushed tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Simmer 10 minutes until slightly thickened.
4. Make 6 wells in the sauce. Crack an egg into each well.
5. Cover and cook 5-8 minutes until egg whites are set but yolks are still runny.
6. Top with feta and fresh herbs. Serve immediately from the skillet with crusty bread.

## Notes

- Yolk doneness: 5-6 minutes for runny, 8-10 for set. Cover traps steam and cooks tops of eggs.
- Spice level: Cayenne is optional. Harissa paste is a more authentic addition.
- Add-ins: Chorizo, merguez, or crispy chickpeas for protein.
- Feta: Not traditional but common. Goat cheese works too.
- The bread: Non-negotiable. You need something to soak up the sauce.
