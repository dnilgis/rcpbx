# Chicken Noodle Soup

Classic comfort. 30 minutes with rotisserie chicken.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 20 min · Serves: 6

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 3 carrots, sliced into half-moons
- 3 celery stalks, sliced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 8 cups chicken broth (good quality)
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp fresh thyme (or ½ tsp dried)
- 1 bay leaf
- 8 oz egg noodles
- 3 cups shredded rotisserie chicken
- 2 tbsp fresh dill or parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Steps

1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
2. Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in broth. Add thyme and bay leaf. Bring to a boil.
4. Add noodles. Cook according to package directions (usually 8-10 minutes) until tender.
5. Stir in shredded chicken. Heat through, about 2 minutes.
6. Remove bay leaf. Stir in fresh dill. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

## Notes

- Broth matters: Use the best quality broth you can find. It's the backbone of the soup.
- From scratch: For richer broth, simmer chicken wings with the vegetables for 1 hour before adding noodles.
- Noodle tip: If making ahead, store noodles separately. They'll absorb liquid and get mushy.
- Storage: Keeps 4 days refrigerated. Freeze broth and chicken without noodles.
