Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Five ingredients. Fifteen minutes. Italian perfection.

Prep: 5 min Cook: 15 min Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup pasta water
  • Parmesan for serving (optional, not traditional)

Instructions

  1. Cook spaghetti in well-salted water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat.
  3. Add sliced garlic and red pepper flakes. Cook slowly 4-5 minutes, stirring often, until garlic is light golden. Don't let it brown.
  4. Remove skillet from heat when garlic is ready.
  5. Add drained pasta directly to the skillet. Toss with 1/2 cup pasta water.
  6. Add parsley and salt. Toss vigorously to emulsify the oil and pasta water into a silky sauce.
  7. Serve immediately with more parsley and parmesan if desired.

Notes

  • Low and slow garlic: This is the whole dish. Burnt garlic = bitter dish. Take your time.
  • Pasta water: The starch is essential for creating the sauce. Don't skip it.
  • Good olive oil: You'll taste it. Use the best you have.
  • Add protein: Anchovies, shrimp, or breadcrumbs are traditional additions.
  • Technique: The tossing action emulsifies the oil and water. Keep the pasta moving.
