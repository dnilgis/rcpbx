# Honey Garlic Chicken

Sticky, sweet, savory. The sauce caramelizes into something magical.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 25 min · Serves: 4

## Ingredients

- 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 8 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup honey
- 1/4 cup soy sauce (or coconut aminos)
- 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 Tbsp sriracha (optional)
- Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken skin-side down. Cook 5-6 minutes until skin is golden and crispy. Flip and cook 2 minutes more. Remove to a plate.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Whisk in honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sriracha (if using). Bring to a simmer.
6. Return chicken to the skillet, skin-side up. Spoon sauce over the chicken.
7. Transfer to oven. Bake 15-18 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F, basting with sauce halfway through.
8. Let rest 5 minutes. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions. Serve with rice.

## Notes

- Crispy skin: Don't move the chicken while it's searing. Let it sit undisturbed to crisp.
- Boneless option: Use boneless thighs and reduce oven time to 10-12 minutes.
- The sauce: It thickens as it cools. Add a splash of water when reheating leftovers.
- Make it hotter: Add more sriracha or a few dashes of red pepper flakes.
- Meal prep friendly: Stores well for 4 days. Reheat in the oven to keep skin crispy.
