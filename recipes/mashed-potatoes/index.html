Mashed Potatoes

Creamy, buttery, never gluey. One crucial tip makes the difference.

Prep 10 min
Cook 25 min
Serves 8

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into 1" chunks
  • 1½ sticks butter (12 tbsp)
  • 1½ cups half-and-half (or 1 cup milk + ½ cup cream)
  • 2 tsp salt, plus more for cooking water
  • Black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Place potatoes in large pot. Cover with cold water by 1 inch. Add 1 tablespoon salt.
  2. Bring to boil over high heat. Reduce to medium, simmer until fork-tender, 20-25 minutes.
  3. While potatoes cook, warm butter and half-and-half in small saucepan until butter melts. Keep warm.
  4. Drain potatoes. Return to pot over low heat for 1 minute to dry out any excess moisture.
  5. For smooth: Pass through ricer or food mill into large bowl. For chunky: Mash with potato masher.
  6. Add BUTTER FIRST. Fold in until absorbed. This coats the starch and prevents glueyness.
  7. Then fold in warm half-and-half gradually until desired consistency.
  8. Season with salt and pepper. Serve immediately.

Notes

BUTTER FIRST: This is the secret. Fat coats starch granules, preventing gluey texture. Don't add milk first.

Yukon Gold: Naturally buttery flavor and creamy texture. Russets work but are starchier.

Use a ricer: Creates smooth potatoes without overworking. Mashing can make them gluey.

Warm the dairy: Cold liquid cools potatoes and makes them gummy. Always warm first.

Make ahead: Hold in slow cooker on warm up to 2 hours. Add splash of cream when serving.
