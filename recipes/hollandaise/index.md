# Hollandaise Sauce

The silky, lemony butter sauce that makes eggs Benedict legendary.

Prep: 5 min · Cook: 10 min · Makes: 1 cup

## Ingredients

- 3 large egg yolks
- 1 Tbsp lemon juice
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) butter, melted and hot
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1 Tbsp warm water (if needed)

## Steps

1. Whisk egg yolks and lemon juice in a heatproof bowl until slightly thickened.
2. Set bowl over a pot of barely simmering water (don't let bowl touch water).
3. Whisk constantly until yolks are warm and slightly thickened, about 2 minutes.
4. Remove from heat. Very slowly drizzle in hot melted butter, whisking constantly.
5. Add butter drop by drop at first, then in a thin stream as sauce emulsifies.
6. Whisk in cayenne and salt. Add warm water if too thick.
7. Serve immediately or keep warm over barely simmering water up to 30 minutes.

## Notes

- Temperature is everything: Too hot = scrambled eggs. Too cool = won't emulsify. Keep it gentle.
- Slow with the butter: Add it drop by drop at first. Rushing causes breaking.
- Broken sauce fix: Whisk a fresh yolk in a clean bowl, slowly whisk in the broken sauce.
- Blender method: Blend yolks and lemon juice, then slowly stream in hot butter with blender running.
- Don't hold too long: Best served immediately. It doesn't reheat well.
