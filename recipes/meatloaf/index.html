Meatloaf

Moist inside, caramelized outside. The sheet pan method.

Prep: 15 min Cook: 1 hr Serves: 8

Ingredients

  • Meatloaf: 2 lbs ground beef (80/20), 1 small onion grated (not diced), 3 garlic cloves minced, ¾ cup panko breadcrumbs, ½ cup milk, 2 eggs, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper, 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Glaze: ½ cup ketchup, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and set a wire rack on top (or just use foil).
  2. Mix panko and milk in large bowl. Let sit 5 minutes (this is the panade).
  3. Add beef, grated onion, garlic, eggs, Worcestershire, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Mix gently with hands until just combined. Don't overwork.
  4. Shape into a free-form loaf on prepared pan, about 9x5 inches.
  5. Bake 40 minutes.
  6. While baking, mix glaze ingredients.
  7. Remove from oven, brush with half the glaze. Increase oven to 400°F.
  8. Bake 15-20 more minutes until internal temp reaches 160°F. Brush with remaining glaze halfway through.
  9. Rest 10 minutes before slicing.

Notes

  • GRATE the onion: Releases moisture without chunks. Keeps meatloaf incredibly moist.
  • Free-form on sheet pan: Fat drains away, all sides get browned. Loaf pans trap grease and steam the meat.
  • The panade: Milk-soaked breadcrumbs keep it tender. Same technique as meatballs.
  • Italian sausage variation: Use 1 lb beef + 1 lb Italian sausage for more flavor.
  • Storage: Keeps 4 days refrigerated. Makes excellent sandwiches cold.
