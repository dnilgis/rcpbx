# Yogurt Tiramisu

The viral Biscoff-yogurt tiramisu. Zero mascarpone, zero regret.

Prep: 15 min (plus overnight chill) · Cook: 0 min · Serves: 6

**Verdict: WORTH IT** — The rare viral dessert that's better than its video - and it's four ingredients.

_Tested 2026-07. Two-second dunks and an overnight chill are the whole game - longer dunks collapse, shorter chills taste like a parfait._

## Ingredients

- 2 cups (450g) full-fat Greek yogurt (5% - this is not the place for 0%)
- 3 Tbsp maple syrup or honey
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of fine salt
- 3/4 cup (180ml) strong brewed coffee or espresso, cooled
- 1 tsp cocoa powder plus more for dusting
- 28-32 Biscoff cookies (about 1 1/2 packs) or 18 ladyfingers
- Dark chocolate, for grating (optional)

## Steps

1. Stir yogurt, maple syrup, vanilla, and salt until smooth. Taste - it should be just sweet enough, not dessert-sweet; the cookies bring plenty.
2. Pour the cooled coffee into a shallow bowl.
3. Dunk cookies one at a time, 1-2 seconds per side - in and out. Biscoff dissolves fast.
4. Lay a layer of dunked cookies in an 8x8 dish. Spread half the yogurt over them.
5. Repeat: dunked cookies, remaining yogurt. Smooth the top.
6. Cover and refrigerate at least 6 hours, ideally overnight. The cookies soften into cake and the layers set.
7. Dust generously with cocoa (and grated chocolate if using) just before serving.

## Notes

- Overnight is the recipe. At hour two it's cookies and yogurt; at hour twelve it's tiramisu.
- Full-fat yogurt reads as cream. Low-fat reads as breakfast.
- Real-deal upgrade: fold 1/2 cup whipped cream into the yogurt for a lighter, richer layer.

## When it goes wrong

- Soggy sludge layer → cookies dunked too long; two seconds means two seconds.
- Tastes like plain yogurt → underchilled or unsalted; overnight, and don't skip the pinch of salt.
- Runny layers → thin yogurt; use thick Greek 5%, or strain regular yogurt an hour in a coffee filter.
- Bitter top → cocoa dusted too early absorbs moisture and clumps; dust at serving time.
