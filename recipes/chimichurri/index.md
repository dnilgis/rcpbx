# Chimichurri

Argentina's iconic herb sauce. Bright, garlicky, and perfect on grilled meat.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 0 min · Makes: 1 cup

## Ingredients

- 1 cup fresh parsley, packed (flat-leaf preferred)
- 4 cloves garlic
- 2 Tbsp fresh oregano (or 2 tsp dried)
- 1/3 cup red wine vinegar
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
- 3/4 cup extra virgin olive oil

## Steps

1. Finely chop parsley, garlic, and oregano by hand or pulse in a food processor. Don't puree—you want texture.
2. Transfer to a bowl. Add red wine vinegar, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Stir to combine.
3. Pour in olive oil and mix well.
4. Let sit at least 10 minutes before serving to let flavors meld. Tastes even better after an hour.

## Notes

- Hand chop vs processor: Hand chopping gives better texture. If using a processor, pulse briefly.
- The vinegar: Red wine vinegar is traditional. Sherry vinegar or lemon juice also work.
- Best on: Grilled steak, chicken, fish, roasted vegetables, crusty bread, eggs.
- Storage: Refrigerate up to 1 week. Bring to room temperature before serving—the oil solidifies when cold.
- Make it ahead: Flavors improve overnight.
