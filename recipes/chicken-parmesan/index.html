Chicken Parmesan

Crispy cutlets, bubbling mozzarella. The key: don't bake it in sauce.

Prep 20 min
Cook 25 min
Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1½ cups panko breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup parmesan, grated
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil for serving

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to broil. Line a baking sheet with foil.
  2. Pound chicken to ¼" even thickness between plastic wrap. This ensures even cooking.
  3. Set up breading station: flour in one dish, beaten eggs in second, panko mixed with parmesan, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and salt in third.
  4. Dredge each cutlet: flour → egg → panko mixture. Press panko firmly to adhere.
  5. Heat oil in large skillet over medium-high. Fry cutlets 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through (165°F). Work in batches.
  6. Transfer cutlets to baking sheet. Spoon marinara around (not over) chicken—keep the breading exposed.
  7. Top each cutlet with mozzarella slices.
  8. Broil 2-3 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly. Watch carefully.
  9. Serve topped with fresh basil and extra sauce on the side.

Notes

The soggy mistake: Most recipes bake chicken IN the sauce. This makes breading soggy. Sauce goes around, not on top.

Pound it thin: ¼" thickness cooks evenly and stays tender. Thick pieces overcook on outside.

Fresh mozzarella: Melts better than shredded, creates better texture.

Make it a sub: Slice and pile on hoagie roll with extra sauce and cheese.

Storage: Best fresh. Reheat on wire rack in 375°F oven to restore crispness.
