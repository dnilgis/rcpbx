# Zucchini Bread

Moist, lightly spiced, and the best way to use summer's zucchini overflow.

Prep: 15 min · Cook: 55 min · Makes: 1 loaf (10 slices)

## Ingredients

- 2 cups shredded zucchini (about 2 medium)
- 3/4 cup sugar
- 1/3 cup vegetable oil
- 2 large eggs
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1/2 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg
- 1/2 cup walnuts, chopped (optional)
- 1/2 cup chocolate chips (optional)

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan.
2. Shred zucchini and squeeze out excess moisture with a clean kitchen towel. You should have about 1 1/2 cups after squeezing.
3. In a large bowl, whisk sugar, oil, eggs, and vanilla until combined.
4. In another bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
5. Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in zucchini and walnuts or chocolate chips if using.
6. Pour batter into prepared pan. Bake 50-60 minutes until a toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.
7. Cool in pan 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely.

## Notes

- Squeeze the zucchini: This is the most important step. Excess moisture = soggy bread.
- Don't overmix: Stir until just combined. Overmixing makes the bread tough.
- Storage: Wrap tightly and store at room temperature 3-4 days, or freeze up to 3 months.
- Double it: This recipe doubles perfectly for two loaves.
- Muffins: Pour into lined muffin tins and bake 20-25 minutes.
