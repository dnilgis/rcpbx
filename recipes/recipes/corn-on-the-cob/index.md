# Corn on the Cob

Summer in a bite. Three ways: boiled, grilled, or roasted.

Prep: 5 min · Cook: 10 min · Serves: 6

## Ingredients

- 6 ears fresh corn, husked
- 4 Tbsp butter
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: chili powder, lime, parmesan, fresh herbs

## Steps

1. BOILED: Bring a large pot of water to boil. Don't add salt—it toughens the corn. Add corn and cook 4-6 minutes until tender. Drain.
2. GRILLED: Preheat grill to medium-high. Brush corn with oil. Grill 10-12 minutes, turning every few minutes, until charred in spots.
3. ROASTED: Preheat oven to 400°F. Place corn directly on oven rack. Roast 20-25 minutes until tender.
4. Serve immediately with butter, salt, and pepper.

## Notes

- Freshness: Fresh corn is best eaten within 1-2 days. The sugar converts to starch over time.
- No salt in water: Salt toughens corn. Season after cooking.
- Test for doneness: Pierce a kernel with your fingernail—it should be tender and release milky juice.
- Mexican street corn (elote): Coat grilled corn with mayo, cotija cheese, chili powder, and lime.
- Cut off the cob: Stand ear upright in a bowl and slice down with a sharp knife.
