# Spaghetti & Meatballs

Tender meatballs, quick marinara. Weeknight-friendly.

Prep: 20 min · Cook: 45 min · Serves: 6

## Ingredients

- Meatballs: 1 lb ground beef (80/20), ½ lb ground pork, ½ cup panko breadcrumbs, ¼ cup milk, 1 egg, ¼ cup parmesan grated, 3 garlic cloves minced, 2 tbsp fresh parsley chopped, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper
- Sauce & Pasta: 28 oz crushed tomatoes, 3 garlic cloves minced, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 lb spaghetti, fresh basil for serving

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line baking sheet with parchment.
2. Make the panade: Mix panko and milk in large bowl. Let sit 5 minutes until absorbed.
3. Add beef, pork, egg, parmesan, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper to bowl. Mix gently with hands until just combined. Don't overwork.
4. Roll into golf ball-sized meatballs (about 20). Place on baking sheet. Bake 15-20 minutes until browned.
5. While meatballs bake, make sauce: Heat oil in large pan over medium. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds. Add tomatoes and Italian seasoning. Simmer 10 minutes.
6. Add baked meatballs to sauce. Simmer together 15-20 minutes.
7. Cook spaghetti according to package. Drain, reserving 1 cup pasta water.
8. Toss pasta with sauce, adding pasta water if needed. Serve meatballs on top with fresh basil.

## Notes

- The panade secret: Soaking breadcrumbs in milk keeps meatballs tender and moist. Don't skip this.
- Why oven-bake? Less messy than pan-frying, cooks evenly, still gets nice browning.
- All beef works: Use 1½ lbs ground beef if you don't have pork.
- Storage: Meatballs keep 4 days refrigerated, freeze beautifully for 3 months.
