# Salmon Rice Bowl

The viral salmon bowl, seared fresh instead of microwaved.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 15 min · Serves: 2

**Verdict: WORTH IT** — The sauce carries it - make double and put it on everything.

_Tested 2026-07. Dropped the false 'crispy rice' claim (nothing crisped the rice), added a salmon pull temp, and credited Mariko's actual method._

## Ingredients

- 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each), skin-on
- 2 cups cooked sushi rice, warm
- 1/2 cup kimchi, chopped
- 1 avocado, sliced
- 2 sheets nori, cut into strips
- 2 green onions, sliced
- Sesame seeds
- 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
- Diamond Crystal kosher salt and black pepper
**For the sauce:**
- 3 Tbsp Kewpie mayo
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sriracha
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1/2 tsp sugar

## Steps

1. Mix all sauce ingredients in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. Pat salmon dry with paper towels. Season skin side with salt. Season flesh side with salt and pepper.
3. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Place salmon skin-side down. Press gently with a spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent curling.
4. Cook 4-5 minutes until skin is deeply crispy and releases easily. Flip and cook 2-3 more minutes - pull at 125°F internal for medium (or 145°F if you want USDA-done).
5. Divide rice between two bowls. Top with kimchi, avocado slices, and nori strips.
6. Place salmon on top, skin-side up so it stays crunchy. Drizzle generously with sauce.
7. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- The crispy skin is the point. Bone-dry salmon, hot pan, don't rush the skin side.
- Kewpie mayo is richer than regular mayo (extra yolk, MSG) - Asian grocery or the international aisle.
- Add a jammy egg (6½-min boil) for extra protein.
- The original: Mariko flakes leftover salmon into rice, microwaves it with an ice cube on top for steam, then adds the same sauce. Works great with leftovers - this recipe is for when you're starting fresh.

## When it goes wrong

- Skin stuck to the pan → fish was wet or pan wasn't hot; dry hard, heat until the oil shimmers, and don't move it early.
- Skin crisp then turned soggy → sat skin-side down in the bowl; always plate skin-up.
- Sauce tastes flat → regular mayo missing the Kewpie richness; add a pinch of sugar and a drop of sesame oil.
