# Penne alla Vodka

Creamy tomato sauce with a kick. The vodka cooks off but the magic stays.

Prep: 10 min · Cook: 25 min · Serves: 4

## Ingredients

- 1 lb penne pasta
- 2 Tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
- 1/2 cup vodka
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup parmesan cheese, grated
- Fresh basil for serving

## Steps

1. Cook penne in salted water until just shy of al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and red pepper flakes. Sauté 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add crushed tomatoes and salt. Simmer 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
4. Remove pan from heat. Add vodka, then return to heat. Simmer 7-8 minutes until sauce reduces and vodka smell dissipates.
5. Reduce heat to low. Stir in heavy cream and simmer 2-3 minutes until sauce turns pink and thickens slightly.
6. Add drained pasta and parmesan. Toss to coat, adding pasta water as needed for consistency.
7. Serve topped with fresh basil and extra parmesan.

## Notes

- Why vodka: It releases flavor compounds in the tomatoes that aren't alcohol or water soluble. The alcohol cooks off.
- Flambé warning: Add vodka off heat to prevent flare-ups, then return to the burner.
- San Marzano: These tomatoes are sweeter and less acidic. Worth seeking out.
- Make it richer: Add 2 Tbsp butter at the end for extra silkiness.
- Add protein: Grilled chicken, Italian sausage, or pancetta all work beautifully.
